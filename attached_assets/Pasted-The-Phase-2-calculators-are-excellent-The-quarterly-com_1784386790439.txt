Step 1: Investments Engine (src/engine/investments.ts)

SWP with Inflation:

Inputs: Total Investment (Corpus), Initial Monthly Withdrawal, Expected Annual Return (%), Expected Annual Inflation (%), Tenure (Years).

Math Logic: The corpus compounds monthly at the expected return. Withdrawals are subtracted monthly. Crucially, at the end of every 12 months, the Monthly Withdrawal amount must increase by the Annual Inflation (%).

Outputs: Total Amount Withdrawn, Final Corpus Value.

Edge Case: If the corpus depletes to ₹0 before the tenure ends, return the exact Month/Year it depleted and stop the calculation.

Retirement Planner:

Inputs: Current Age, Retirement Age, Life Expectancy, Current Monthly Expenses, Expected Inflation (%), Pre-Retirement Return (%), Post-Retirement Return (%), Current Savings (already invested).

Math Logic:

Calculate Future Monthly Expenses at retirement age using the expected inflation.

Calculate the Total Retirement Corpus required to sustain those inflated expenses from Retirement Age until Life Expectancy, assuming the corpus grows at the Post-Retirement Return rate but is depleted by inflation-adjusted withdrawals.

Subtract the Future Value of the Current Savings (compounded at Pre-Retirement Return) from the Total Corpus required.

Calculate the Required Monthly SIP to reach the remaining shortfall.

Outputs: Monthly Expenses at Retirement, Total Corpus Required, Required Monthly SIP.
